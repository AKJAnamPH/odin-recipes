ADOBO RECIPE

Description:

Philippine adobo (from Spanish adobar: "marinade," "sauce" or "seasoning" / English: /əˈdoʊboʊ/ Tagalog pronunciation: [ɐdobo]) is a popular Filipino dish and cooking process in Philippine cuisine that involves meat, seafood, or vegetables marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns, which is browned in oil, and simmered in the marinade. It has occasionally been considered the unofficial national dish in the Philippines.

    Ingredients

  • One whole chicken
  • Soy sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Pepper
  • Sugar
  • Laurel leaves
  • Potatoes
  • Garlic and Onions
  1. For 20 mins. marinate the chicken with soy sauce, vinegar, pepper and sugar.
  2. In a hot pan put oil then saute garlic then onions.
  3. Put the chicken without the sauce one by one until it is golden brown.
  4. Once the oil from the chicken emerged, add the marinated sauce and let simmer.
  5. Taste and add necessary flavor to your liking.
